How to Play

A starting hand is 1 card more than the number of players.

Example: If there are 4 players, each gets dealt 5 cards.

Card values are based on a standard playing deck, as are the suits.


The game begins by each player, in round robin, choosing whether to add a card to the pot or pass.

If playing with physical cards, the player to the dealer's left starts. In this web version, the host player always starts.

This goes around twice, allowing each player to add up to 2 cards to the pot.

Once both rotations of round robin are done, the player that contributes the highest total value to the pot gets to claim it.


Now that a player has claimed the pot, it is the table's chance to crumble their hopes of an easy victory!

The table goes round robin, one player against the House at a time.

The table player plays a card to decide the suit, and the House must beat its value in order to claim that player's slice of the pot.

The House must play within the suit set by the table, otherwise they must play a card at least triple the table's value.


If a tie happens in phase 1, both tied players will become House for the second phase.

Both House players must individually match or beat the table's card. The player that plays the highest card gets to claim the more valuable card from that slice of the pot.

If one House player does not beat the table's card, then the other gets to claim both cards from the pot.

If a ties happens again in phase 2 between the House players, then the player that played their card first takes the higher value card from the pot.
